Travel & Transport

Airports

Dublin: Regular service, often direct by Aer Lingus, and most major U.S. Airlines. Great roads, 2hr drive. Bus connections.
Cork: Norwegian Airlines fly from Providence, RI. Moderate roads, 1hr drive. Bus connections.
Shannon: Norwegian Airlines fly from New York - Stewart. Crap roads, ~2hr 30min drive.

Rental Cars

By far the best option for transport - public transport links may limit ability to see some of the country. All major vendors are at all airports - Cian rents with Hertz and Enterprise frequently. Expect to pay 1.5x for an automatic over a manual car.

Busses

Good public transport links exist between Dublin and Dungarvan. JJ Kavanagh & Bus Eireann operate routes - change in Waterford City, total journey time ~4hrs.

Aer Lingus

Aer Lingus are Ireland's national airline. They fly to both Cork and Shannon from a whole host of U.S. destinations, including twice daily to & from Logan to Dublin. They have reasonable rates, and include in-flight meals. The Shannon route tends to use older aircraft.

Norwegian Air

Norwegian fly from both Providence and New York Stewart to Dublin, Cork and Shannon. Their rates are very cheap, but they are a budget airline with no in-flight meals.

Other

United, Delta and American offer direct routes to Dublin and Shannon from some U.S. cities. These sometimes codeshare with Aer Lingus.
